WHAT IS SIDING & WHY DO I NEED IT?

The primary importance of siding is to protect the overall health of your home. The natural elements including rain, snow and wind take a beating on your home so having high quality siding is crucial. Additionally, new siding that adds a brand new look and feel to your home as a whole. There are a wide range of other options, but the truth is that your siding should look nice on the outside and it should be doing its job protecting everyone on the inside.

When people look to upgrade their home, they often look towards interior additions and improvements BUT one of the most important aspects of a home that people tend to forget and/or pass on is their siding... But what is it? What is the importance of siding? What does it do? And how do I know if I need it or if I need to replace it?


  • Does your home need frequent painting?
  • Have your heating/cooling bills spiked lately?
  • Have you noticed any rotting and/or warping on the exterior of your home?
  • Have you seen fungus, mold or mildew growth?

If you answered yes to any of those questions, give us a call!

We won't just put any regular old siding on your home. We're a certified James Hardie siding installer! Why does that matter?

    Compared to vinyl siding, James Hardie fiber cement siding and trim are:


  • More attractive – over five times thicker than vinyl so grooves appear deeper with a more authentic wood-grain effect.
  • Noncombustible, more durable, and resist warping, sagging and melting.
  • Resist moisture and mold
  • Withstand damage much better

    Compared to wood siding, James Hardie fiber cement siding and trim:


  • Resists the effects of moisture and damage from mold.
  • Withstands fire damage while wood goes up in flames.
  • Resists weather damage and keeps shape much longer while wood may split, crack and deteriorate over time.
  • Provides more protection than wood from wet and freezing conditions as well as hot, humid weather.

    Compared to engineered wood siding, James Hardie fiber cement siding and trim are:


  • Specially formulated to better resist damage from the very predators—moisture, freezing temperature, humidity, pests and fire—that pose threats to wood-based products.
  • Don’t expand and buckle
